Number of Songs in Each Musical Key Bar Chart

This visualization is interactive! Hover over a bar to see the number of songs in that key.

Description

The bar chart displays the distribution of songs across musical keys, categorized by mode (major or minor). Each key is represented numerically from 0 to 11, corresponding to the 12 chromatic scale pitches: 0 = C, 1 = C♯/D♭, 2 = D, 3 = D♯/E♭, 4 = E, 5 = F, 6 = F♯/G♭, 7 = G, 8 = G♯/A♭, 9 = A, 10 = A♯/B♭, and 11 = B. These values denote the root note of each key, and the chart reflects how frequently songs occur in each key-mode pairing. Keys 0 (C), 1 (C♯/D♭), and 8 (G♯/A♭) each feature 13 songs, the highest among all keys, though their mode distributions differ significantly. Keys 0 and 8 favor the major key, while key 1 is nearly balanced (7 major, 6 minor). Key 6 (F♯/G♭) is the only other with a near-even split (5 major, 6 minor). Most keys show a clear preference for major mode, especially 2 (D), 7 (G), and 8 (G♯/A♭). Keys 5 and 10 are two of the few keys that favor minor. Overall, major keys dominate, but minor keys remain a significant and stylistically relevant component of the dataset.
